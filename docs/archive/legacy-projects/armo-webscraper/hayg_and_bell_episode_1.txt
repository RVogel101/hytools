Episode 1: The Shadow of the Titan | Տիտանի մը Ստուերը (Expanded Version)

Characters:
*   **NARRATOR** (Պատմող)
*   **HAYG** (Հայկ) - Patriarch of his clan.
*   **NVARD** (Նվարդ) - Hayk's wife.
*   **ARMENAK** (Արմենակ) - Hayk's fiery eldest son.
*   **ELAM** (Եղամ) - A pragmatic elder of Hayk's clan.
*   **PEL** (Պել) - The Titan King of Papelon.
*   **ZIMRI** (Զիմրի) - A sycophantic courtier to Pel.
*   **PEL'S HERALD** (Պելի Աբրահանդը)
*   **TWO LABORERS** (Երկու Բանուորներ)

________________________________________

**English Script**

**(SCENE START)**

**SCENE 1**

**EXT. ZIGGURAT CONSTRUCTION SITE - DAY**

VISUAL: A vast, sun-baked plain. In the center, a massive, unfinished ziggurat tower dominates the skyline of the sprawling city of Papelon. Thousands of LABORERS toil under the harsh sun, hauling bricks. The atmosphere is oppressive and dusty.

NARRATOR: In the age after the great flood, humanity spread across the earth. In the flatlands of Shinar, they built a great city, Papelon. But its greatness was measured in stone and fear, not in spirit.

SOUND: The crack of whips, the groaning of men, the rhythmic chanting of foremen.

Two laborers, an OLD MAN and a YOUNG MAN, struggle to haul a large stone block with ropes.

OLD MAN
(Wheezing, wiping sweat)
Keep pulling. Before the shadows lengthen, or we'll feel the master's whip.

YOUNG MAN
(Voice bitter)
The shadows are always long in Papelon, old man. They are cast by that tower. And by him.

He glances up at the highest balcony of the palace, where a distant, giant figure can be seen.

OLD MAN
Hush, boy. The walls have ears, and the ears belong to Pel. Pull. It is all we can do.

**SCENE 2**

**EXT. HAYG'S QUARTER - DAY**

VISUAL: A quieter part of the city. The architecture is simpler, more functional. HAYG, a man with a powerful build and intelligent eyes, is teaching his young grandson, KADMOS, how to fletch an arrow. His wife, NVARD, watches from the doorway of their home.

HAYG
(Gently guiding the boy's hands)
Patience, Kadmos. The eagle's feather must be bound perfectly straight. An arrow that flies true is an extension of the archer's will. It cannot be rushed.

Nvard approaches, her expression loving but tinged with worry.

NVARD
You teach him the ways of the hunter. But what world will he hunt in? Every day, Pel's guards grow bolder. The market taxes are higher. The children of our neighbors look thinner.

HAYG
(Looks up, his gaze drifting to Pel's tower)
I know. He builds a monument to his own pride, and the people are the mortar. He believes he can command the sun and the stars.

NVARD
And can he?

HAYG
(A low rumble of a voice, bored)
He can command fear. But fear is not faith. A man who must call himself a god is neither. Remember that.

**SCENE 3**

**EXT. CITY SQUARE - MIDDAY**

VISUAL: A royal HERALD, flanked by imposing guards, stands in the city square. He unrolls a scroll. A crowd gathers, a mix of Hayk's people and other city dwellers, their faces weary.

PEL'S HERALD
(Shouting)
By royal decree of the Divine Pel, Lord of Papelon and Master of All He Surveys! A golden statue has been raised in his divine image! All must come! All must bow! All must worship! To refuse is to embrace death!

VISUAL: A wave of fear ripples through the crowd. People murmur, avoiding eye contact with the guards. Hayg is in the crowd, his son ARMENAK beside him. Armenak's hand clenches into a fist.

ARMENAK
(Through gritted teeth)
He commands us to worship metal and stone? To bow to a man?

HAYG
(Placing a calming hand on his son's shoulder)
Watch. Listen. Learn what it is they truly fear.

As others begin to shuffle towards the statue, Hayg remains still, his jaw set in defiance.

**SCENE 4**

**INT. PEL'S THRONE ROOM - DAY**

VISUAL: The throne room is opulent and vast, filled with gold and incense. PEL, a giant of a man, lounges on his throne. His courtier, ZIMRI, a man in fine robes, stands beside him.

ZIMRI
The statue is magnificent, O Divine One. The gold shines like the sun itself. The people will gaze upon it and know that you are their master and their god.

PEL
(A low rumble of a voice, bored)
They know they are my property, Zimri. That is all that matters. This statue... it is a reminder. A brand. A mark of ownership on the soul of this city.

ZIMRI
A brilliant stroke, my lord. They will bow. They always do.

PEL
(A cruel smile)
Let them bow. Let them scrape their faces in the dirt. It amuses me. See to it that everyone complies. Especially that proud hunter, Hayk, and his stubborn clan. I want to see his knees bend.

**SCENE 5**

**INT. HAYG'S HOME - NIGHT**

VISUAL: Hayg is inside his home with his sons, ARMENAK, and the clan elders. A single lamp illuminates their serious faces. The mood is heavy.

ELAM
(An old, cautious elder)
You heard the decree. It is not a request. It is a command backed by swords. We are farmers and hunters, Hayg. His army is a legion.

ARMENAK
So we bow? We teach our children to worship a tyrant because he has more spears than we do? That is a coward's peace, Elam!

ELAM
It is a survivor's peace! Our people have survived famine, flood, and generations of kings by knowing when to bend. A reed that does not bend in the wind, Armenak, is the first to snap!

HAYG
(His voice cuts through the argument, calm but firm)
Elam speaks with the wisdom of years. And Armenak with the fire of truth. Both are valuable. But the wind he speaks of has changed. This is not a wind that wishes to bend us. This is a storm that seeks to uproot us entirely.

He stands, his shadow looming large on the wall.

HAYG
He does not ask for taxes or labor. He asks for our souls. He demands we betray the faith of our ancestors, the memory of the Creator who saved us from the flood. If we give him that, we have nothing left. To live on our knees is not living. It is a slow death.

He looks at each person in the room, his gaze filled with conviction.

HAYG
We cannot stay. I will not let my people become hollow shells. We will go north, to the high lands of Ararat that our fathers spoke of. The journey will be perilous, but it is a path towards life. Here, there is only a gilded cage. We will find our own land, and build our own home. A home where we can stand tall.

VISUAL: Close up on Hayg's determined face, lit by the lamp's flame. The flame flickers, reflecting the spark of rebellion in his eyes.

**(SCENE END)**
