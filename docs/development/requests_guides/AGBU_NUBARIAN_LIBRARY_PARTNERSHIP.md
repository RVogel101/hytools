# AGBU Nubar Library Partnership and Access

The AGBU Nubar Library (Bibliothèque Nubar) in Paris holds 43,000+ printed books, 800,000 archival documents, 1,400 periodicals, and 10,000 photographs. It is a major resource for Western Armenian diaspora studies.

**Website:** [https://bnulibrary.org](https://bnulibrary.org)  
**Location:** 11 Square Alboni, 75016 Paris, France  
**Access:** Open by appointment only

---

## Step-by-Step Access Plan

### 1. Explore the Digital Collection

- Visit **[https://bnulibrary.org](https://bnulibrary.org)**
- Browse digitized collections: [Collections photos](https://bnulibrary.org/index.php/fr/collections-photos), [Archives](https://bnulibrary.org/index.php/fr/archives), [Périodiques](https://bnulibrary.org/index.php/fr/periodiques)
- Renaissance (1918–1920), Le Foyer, Andonian archives, and virtual exhibitions are already online

### 2. Identify What You Need

- **Printed books:** 43,000 volumes (Constantinople/Istanbul, Venice editions)
- **Periodicals:** 1,400 collections (Ottoman Armenian press)
- **Archives:** Patriarchate of Istanbul, Andonian genocide testimonies, AGBU, Armenian National Delegation

### 3. Contact the Library

- **Website:** [https://bnulibrary.org](https://bnulibrary.org) — use the contact form or info on the site
- **In person:** 11 Square Alboni, 75016 Paris (by appointment)
- **AGBU main:** [https://agbu.org](https://agbu.org) — may route to library for partnership inquiries

### 4. Request Partnership or Access

**For a research partnership (e.g. bulk digitization, OCR collaboration):**

- Describe your project: Western Armenian language corpus for NLP research
- Specify: interest in digitized text, periodicals, or archival materials
- Mention: non-commercial, academic use, proper attribution
- Reference: Calfa Project (2018) — partnership for OCR software for Armenian materials ([https://calfa.fr/blog/7](https://calfa.fr/blog/7))

**For on-site research:**

- Request an appointment
- Specify dates and materials you wish to consult
- Bring institutional affiliation

### 5. Partnership Models (from Calfa example)

- **Library supplies:** High-definition digital images of manuscripts, archives, letters
- **You supply:** OCR processing, technical expertise, or corpus building
- **Outcome:** Joint improvement of Armenian accessibility and preservation

### 6. If No Direct Contact Found

- **AGBU Resources:** [https://agbu.org/resources](https://agbu.org/resources)
- **AGBU Press:** Contact for general AGBU inquiries; they may forward to the library
- **Études arméniennes contemporaines:** The library’s journal; editorial contact may connect you to library staff

## Draft Email: Partnership / Access Request

Use the text below (customize name, affiliation, and dates) when contacting the library via the website contact form or AGBU.

---

**Subject:** Research partnership request — Western Armenian corpus & digitized collections

Dear AGBU Nubar Library team,

I am writing to request the possibility of a research partnership or formal access to your digitized collections for a Western Armenian language corpus project.

**Project:** We are building an open-source, non-commercial corpus of Western Armenian text for natural language processing (NLP) research and language preservation. The goal is to support tools for Western Armenian (lexicons, grammar checkers, and eventually language models), with full attribution to all contributing institutions.

**Interest in your collections:** The Nubar Library's holdings—especially periodicals (1,400 collections), printed books (Constantinople/Istanbul and Venice editions), and archival materials—are an invaluable resource for historical and contemporary Western Armenian. We are particularly interested in:
- Digitized text or OCR-ready materials from your periodicals and books
- Any existing bulk exports or APIs, or the possibility of structured access for research use

**Usage:** All data would be used only for non-commercial, academic research and preservation. We would provide full attribution to the Bibliothèque Nubar / AGBU in publications and in the corpus metadata, and would respect any terms you set (e.g. no redistribution of raw images, citation requirements).

**Partnership model:** We are aware of the Calfa Project (2018) collaboration, in which the library supplied high-definition images and the partner contributed OCR and technical expertise. We would be glad to discuss a similar arrangement—e.g. we could contribute OCR processing, corpus-building pipelines, or quality control in exchange for access to digitized materials—or a simpler permission for research use of existing digital content.

**Next steps:** Could you advise whether such a partnership or research access is possible, and whom we should contact for a formal request (e.g. partnership agreement, MOU, or terms of use)?

Thank you for your work in preserving and providing access to Armenian cultural heritage.

## Notes

- The library serves researchers, students, journalists, and artists “without restrictions” for on-site use
- Digitization is ongoing; new materials are added to the website
- For bulk corpus building, a formal partnership or MOU may be needed for redistribution of digitized content
